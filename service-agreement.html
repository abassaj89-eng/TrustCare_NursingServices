Participant: The NDIS participant receiving clinical nursing supports (details specified in the individual agreement).

1. About TrustCare Support

TrustCare Support is an AHPRA-registered Registered Nurse (RN) service. We are not an NDIS registered provider. We operate as a specialised clinical nursing partner engaged by registered NDIS providers to deliver the nursing component of a participant's plan.

NDIS funding for TrustCare's services is billed through the registered provider partner — not directly through TrustCare. Only participants who are self-managed or plan-managed may engage TrustCare directly.

2. Scope of Nursing Supports

TrustCare Support provides the following clinical nursing services (specific supports are confirmed in each individual service agreement):

  • Complex wound assessment and management
  • Catheter and continence care (indwelling, suprapubic, and external catheter management)
  • Continence assessments and care planning
  • Enteral feeding management (PEG and PEJ)
  • Tracheostomy care and airway management support
  • Subcutaneous medication administration (including insulin)
  • Medication management and administration support
  • Complex bowel care
  • Epilepsy and seizure management planning and support
  • Diabetes management and monitoring
  • Dysphagia and mealtime management support
  • Ventilator and respiratory support
  • High-Intensity Daily Personal Activities (HIDPA) training and competency assessment for support workers
  • Clinical governance and compliance consulting for NDIS providers
  • Clinical assessments and nursing care planning

All services are delivered exclusively by, or under the supervision of, an AHPRA-registered Registered Nurse. All care is delivered within the defined scope of practice of a Registered Nurse in accordance with the NMBA Registered Nurse Standards for Practice (2016).

3. NDIS Funding, Pricing and Fees

TrustCare Support aligns its pricing with the NDIS Pricing Arrangements and Price Limits published by the NDIA. Specific fees, NDIS support item numbers and rates will be confirmed in the individual service agreement prior to commencement of services.

  • Registered Nurse supports are typically funded under the NDIS support category Daily Activities — High Intensity or Capacity Building — Support Coordination, depending on the nature of the support.
  • Fees are charged per hour or per session as agreed in the individual service agreement.
  • Travel costs may apply for in-home service delivery. Any travel charges will be agreed in advance and are consistent with NDIS Pricing Arrangements.
  • Invoices are issued to the registered provider partner or plan manager as agreed.
  • Payment terms are 14 days from invoice date unless otherwise agreed.

NDIS funding is the responsibility of the registered provider partner. TrustCare does not make representations about a participant's funding eligibility or plan balance.

4. Service Delivery

TrustCare Support will:

  • Deliver all nursing supports as described in this agreement and the individual service plan
  • Maintain active AHPRA registration for all nurses delivering services under this agreement
  • Comply with the NDIS Code of Conduct in all interactions with participants and their representatives
  • Deliver services with respect for the participant's dignity, privacy, and individual goals
  • Maintain clear clinical documentation and provide service reports to the registered provider partner within 48 hours of each visit
  • Communicate openly and promptly with the participant's treating GP, allied health team, and care coordinator
  • Review the care plan at least annually or when there is a significant change in the participant's clinical needs

5. Cancellation Policy

TrustCare Support's cancellation policy is consistent with the NDIS Pricing Arrangements short notice cancellation provisions.

Short Notice Cancellation (by participant or registered provider):

  • A cancellation is considered short notice if TrustCare is notified less than 2 business days before the scheduled appointment.
  • For short notice cancellations, TrustCare may charge up to 100% of the agreed support fee in accordance with NDIS Pricing Arrangements.
  • Cancellations due to illness or a genuine emergency will be considered on a case-by-case basis.

Standard Cancellation (by participant or registered provider):

  • Cancellations with more than 2 business days' notice — no charge applies.

Cancellation by TrustCare:

  • TrustCare will provide as much notice as possible of any cancellation on our part.
  • We will take all reasonable steps to reschedule or arrange an alternative clinical resource.

6. Variation and Termination of this Agreement

Variation: This agreement may be varied at any time by agreement between both parties in writing. Either party may request a review of the agreement at any time.

Termination by the participant or registered provider: Either party may end this agreement at any time by giving 14 days' written notice. TrustCare will continue to provide agreed services during the notice period unless the safety of the participant or staff is at risk.

Termination by TrustCare: TrustCare will provide 14 days' written notice prior to ending this agreement, except where continued service delivery presents an unacceptable clinical or safety risk. In such cases, TrustCare will take all reasonable steps to support continuity of care through the registered provider partner.

7. Provider Responsibilities

TrustCare Support is committed to:

  • Delivering supports that are safe, high quality, and consistent with AHPRA and NMBA standards
  • Complying with the NDIS Code of Conduct
  • Maintaining current professional indemnity and public liability insurance
  • Protecting participant privacy and handling personal and health information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles
  • Maintaining a current incident register and responding to incidents in accordance with our Incident Management Statement
  • Notifying the registered provider partner promptly of any incident, concern, or change in a participant's clinical condition
  • Providing all staff with appropriate clinical supervision and professional development
  • Working cooperatively with the participant, their registered provider, care coordinator, and treating team

8. Participant Rights

Under this agreement and the NDIS framework, you have the right to:

  • Be treated with dignity and respect at all times
  • Make informed decisions about your own care and to give or withhold consent to any clinical intervention
  • Exercise choice and control over how and when supports are delivered
  • Have your privacy and confidentiality protected
  • Have a support person, advocate, or authorised representative present during any care or assessment
  • Receive information in a format and language accessible to you
  • Make a complaint at any time without fear of reprisal — see our Complaints Policy
  • Change provider or end this agreement with 14 days' notice
  • Contact the NDIS Quality and Safeguards Commission directly at any time: 📞 1800 035 544 | 🌐 ndiscommission.gov.au

These rights are in addition to your rights under the NDIS Act 2013 (Cth), the Disability Services Act 1993 (WA), and Australian Consumer Law.

9. Privacy and Confidentiality

TrustCare handles all personal and health information in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles. Health information is sensitive information and is only collected, used, and disclosed as necessary to deliver nursing services or as required by law.

Our full Privacy Policy is available on our website. By signing a service agreement, participants consent to TrustCare collecting and using health information as described in the Privacy Policy.
