Registered Nurses play a central role in diabetes management for complex NDIS participants. Key responsibilities include: developing and reviewing individualised diabetes management plans; training support workers in blood glucose monitoring technique and documentation; administering insulin and other injectable medications where required; and responding to hypoglycaemic and hyperglycaemic episodes.

Hypoglycaemia recognition is a critical safety competency for any support worker assisting a participant with diabetes. Warning signs include shakiness, sweating, confusion, and pallor. All staff must know the emergency response protocol and when to escalate to an RN or emergency services. TrustCare Support provides diabetes-specific training and RN-led management plans for NDIS participants in Perth.
