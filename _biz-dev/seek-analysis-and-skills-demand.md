## Executive Summary

Analysis of NDIS nursing job advertisements in Perth WA (November 2025 – March 2026) across Seek, Indeed, Jora, and employer career pages reveals a persistently undersupplied market for AHPRA-registered nurses across all High Intensity Daily Personal Activity (HIDPA) skill areas. The subcontractor model — engaging an RN entity rather than employing an individual nurse — is underutilised in Perth NDIS but directly analogous to the established Flame Lily model operating in NSW aged care, and is well-suited to TrustCare's B2B positioning.

**Bottom line:** TrustCare's current service offering matches or exceeds what Perth's NDIS providers are advertising for. The gaps are in marketing reach and contract vehicles — not clinical capability.

### Subcontractor Rate Analysis

The spread between what providers bill ($123.65/hr RN weekday) and what they pay employed nurses (~$51/hr) leaves **$72.65/hr gross margin** per weekday RN hour to cover the provider's overheads, coordination, rostering, and profit.

A subcontractor arrangement with TrustCare operating at an agreed rate (typically $85–$100/hr as an ABN entity) still leaves the lead provider meaningful margin for care coordination, while TrustCare retains a sustainable operating position without the overhead of large-scale employment.

**Implication for TrustCare's B2B pitch:** the economics of subcontracting are favourable for both parties at any agreed rate between $80–$110/hr for TrustCare.

## 5. Employee vs Contractor/Subcontractor Model

### What Providers Currently Do

| Model | Providers Using It | Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| **Permanent employee RN/EN** | Brightwater, GAHSS, Adelphi Living, SN Services | High turnover (17–26%/yr), recruitment costs ($2,130–$3,320/hire), not flexible to participant demand |
| **Casual pool** | Most large providers | Inconsistency of care, HIDPA competency currency tracking complexity |
| **External agency** | Growing use | Expensive (30–50% agency margin); no clinical governance continuity |
| **Subcontractor (clinical entity)** | Rare in Perth NDIS; established in NSW aged care (Flame Lily model) | **Underexploited gap** — where TrustCare sits |

### Why the Subcontractor Model Wins for Perth Providers

1. **No NDIS price cap erosion from overhead** — TrustCare bills under the participant's plan via the registered provider; no additional operational budget required from the provider
2. **HIDPA compliance without headcount** — TrustCare's RN signs off on worker competencies as part of the subcontract relationship; the provider gets the documentation without hiring the nurse
3. **Surge and specialisation capacity** — tracheostomy/ventilation/VDQ nursing is not viable to maintain in-house for most mid-size providers; subcontracting specialist clinical capability is more cost-effective
4. **Risk offloading** — TrustCare holds its own PI/PL insurance, AHPRA registration, and worker screening; the registered provider's risk exposure for clinical incidents is reduced under a properly structured subcontract

## 7. Key Recommendations for TrustCare

**1. Lead with tracheostomy and ventilation in outbound marketing**
These are the #1 and #2 most-sought skills in Perth NDIS nursing job ads. Providers are actively paying agency rates to fill these gaps. TrustCare's capability here should be front and centre in the value proposition document and website hero.

**2. Add paediatric complex nursing language to the website**
SN Services and At Home Care are both actively marketing paediatric complex care. If TrustCare delivers this, adding explicit paediatric capability (with appropriate caveats) to the site would capture a high-value underserved segment.

**3. Add SCI and ABI as explicit participant conditions served**
These are the most common complex participant profiles generating nursing demand in Perth. A support coordinator searching for "NDIS nursing spinal cord injury Perth" or "NDIS ABI nurse Perth" would not currently find TrustCare.

**4. Market the subcontractor economics**
Show providers the maths: NDIS bills participant plan at $123.65/hr; TrustCare delivers the clinical care as a subcontract; the provider retains the coordination margin with no staffing overhead. No comparable provider is making this case explicitly.

**5. Target the competency training service to providers who are using support workers for HIDPA**
Interchange WA is explicitly training support workers in PEG, seizures, and diabetes — this is exactly the provider who needs TrustCare's formal RN competency sign-off service to remain compliant with the February 2025 Skills Descriptor changes.

**6. Clarify on-call and 24-hour access**
SN Services' biggest differentiator is 24/7 on-call CNS access. If TrustCare can offer after-hours clinical phone consultation (not necessarily in-person), this closes a competitive gap and is a strong trust signal for SIL providers managing high-acuity participants overnight.
