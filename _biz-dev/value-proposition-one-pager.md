## The Problem Your Organisation Is Facing

Western Australia's NDIS nursing workforce has reached a structural crisis point.

- **Over 80% of WA disability providers** report being unable to access the services their participants need due to workforce shortages *(NDS WA Pre-Budget Submission 2025-26)*
- Australia faces a shortage of **85,000–123,000 registered nurses** by 2025–2030 *(VU Online; Talent Hub Australia 2025)*
- The NDIS sector requires **128,000 additional workers by mid-2025** — clinical roles are among the hardest to fill *(NDIS Review, May 2023)*
- Annual NDIS workforce turnover is **17–26%**, costing providers $2,130–$3,320 per replacement hire and over **$50 million sector-wide per year** *(NDS Workforce Census Report 2025)*
- **55.7% of providers reported financial losses** in 2023–24, with staffing consuming 81.5% of provider revenue

For registered NDIS providers delivering complex care or high intensity supports, the gap between participant need and available AHPRA-registered clinical nursing is the single biggest operational constraint — and it is getting worse.

## NDIS Pricing Context

Clinical nursing delivered by TrustCare is billed under your participant's NDIS plan — it does not come from your organisation's operational budget.

| Service | NDIS Registration Group | 2025-26 National Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Registered Nurse — weekday daytime | 0114 (item 01_606_0114_1_1) | **$123.65/hr** |
| Registered Nurse — Saturday | 0114 (item 01_608_0114_1_1) | **$176.47/hr** |
| High Intensity Support (worker) — weekday | 0107 (item 01_010_0107_1_1) | **$76.09/hr** |

Participants whose plans include community nursing care (Group 0114) or high intensity daily personal activities (Group 0107) have funding specifically allocated for these supports. Engaging TrustCare unlocks utilisation of that existing plan funding — no new funding category required for most participants.
