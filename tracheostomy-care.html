What Is Tracheostomy Support Under the NDIS?

Tracheostomy support is one of the nine High Intensity Daily Personal Activities (HIDPAs) under NDIS Practice Standards Module 1, and is classified as a Level 3 support — the highest risk category alongside ventilator care. It applies to participants with a permanent or long-term tracheostomy tube who require assistance with tracheostomy management in a community setting.

For registered NDIS providers, this means a Registered Nurse must be involved before any support worker delivers tracheostomy care — and that RN must develop a participant-specific care plan, train each worker individually, and formally sign off on their demonstrated competency.

NDIS Funding for Tracheostomy Care

Tracheostomy care is funded under two NDIS registration groups depending on who delivers the care:

  • Community Nursing Care — Registered Nurse (Group 0114): $123.65/hr weekday daytime nationally (2025-26) — applies when our RN delivers direct clinical care
  • High Intensity Daily Personal Activities (Group 0107): $76.09/hr weekday daytime — applies when a trained and delegated support worker delivers routine tracheostomy care under RN supervision

Both are funded from the participant's NDIS plan — they do not come from the registered provider's operational budget.

Compliance Requirements (February 2025)

Under the NDIS High Intensity Support Skills Descriptors (effective February 2025), tracheostomy support requires:

  • A participant-specific care plan developed by a qualified health practitioner (RN)
  • Documented competency assessment for each worker — demonstrated competency, not just course attendance
  • Annual competency review, or earlier if the participant's needs change or a worker has been absent from the support for 3+ months
  • If the participant is also on ventilator support: workers must hold competency across both the tracheostomy AND ventilator skill descriptors
